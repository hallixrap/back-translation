You should be able to use the toilet too without needing a lot of help. You will need to pay attention to not dislocate your artificial hip, especially in the first few months after the operation. You will need to learn exercises that make your new hip stronger and take special precautions. You will need a person with you at home for 1 to 2 weeks after you leave the hospital or the rehabilitation center. You will need help to prepare food, bathe, move around in the house, and do other activities every day. With time, you should be able to return to the level of activity you had before. You will need to avoid some sports, like mountain skiing or contact sports like football and soccer. But you should be able to do activities that are not strong, like hiking, gardening, swimming, playing tennis, and golfing.
